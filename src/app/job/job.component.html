Innovation Solutions Intern

Jan-April 2019
Purolator

Worked on a proof of concept in a group of 2 other coops; we wanted to find a solution to easily digitize documents, specifically filled in bills of lading. The bills were forms that had key pairs we wanted to match up, for example "country: CA, phone number: (xxx)-xxx-xxxx," etc. that would be collected and sent through an api to be added into their existing database. The challenge was that there was no default form layout, and the needed information were in different formats, locations, and various wording/synonyms.

We started off by researching various OCR technologies, and came to a final decision between Google's Cloud Vision API and IBM's BACA (Business Automation Content Analyzer). Although Google's solution had better base capabilities (it supported handwriting and 2d barcodes), it did not provide any post processing to recognize any of the key pairs by itself, which we considered as a major setback.

The rest of the time was spent working with IBM's support team and within our group to optimize the program for best results in its post-processing step, but it was concluded that many of the critical functionalities we required was planned to be devleloped but not yet implemented. Despite our best efforts, we could not reach our predetermined definition of success before the end of the term.
